Standing Poses

Standing poses build strength and stability, improving balance and posture. They engage multiple muscle groups and enhance focus.

Examples: Tadasana (Mountain Pose), Virabhadrasana (Warrior Pose), Trikonasana (Triangle Pose).

  • Improves posture and balance
  • Strengthens legs and core
  • Increases focus and body awareness
  • Enhances flexibility in hips and spine

Seated Poses

Seated poses promote flexibility and relaxation, calming the mind and reducing stress. They often serve as a preparation for meditation.

Examples: Sukhasana (Easy Pose), Padmasana (Lotus Pose), Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend).

  • Enhances flexibility in the spine and legs
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Promotes relaxation and stress relief
  • Supports meditation practices

Forward Bends

Forward bends help stretch the back and hamstrings while promoting relaxation and introspection. They can also reduce anxiety and stress.

Examples: Uttanasana (Standing Forward Bend), Janu Sirsasana (Head-to-Knee Pose), Balasana (Child's Pose).

  • Stretches the spine and hamstrings
  • Calms the mind and relieves stress
  • Promotes introspection and relaxation
  • Improves digestion

Backbends

Backbends open the chest and improve spinal flexibility. They energize the body and can help counteract the effects of sitting.

Examples: Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose), Urdhva Dhanurasana (Upward Bow Pose), Dhanurasana (Bow Pose).

  • Increases spinal flexibility
  • Opens the chest and shoulders
  • Boosts energy and alleviates fatigue
  • Improves lung capacity

Twists

Twists promote spinal flexibility and aid in digestion. They help detoxify the body and can alleviate back pain.

Examples: Ardha Matsyendrasana (Half Lord of the Fishes Pose), Bharadvajasana (Bharadvaja's Twist), Marichyasana (Marichi's Pose).

  • Improves digestion and detoxification
  • Increases spinal mobility
  • Relieves tension in the back and shoulders
  • Reduces back pain

Balancing Poses

Balancing poses challenge stability and focus, helping to improve coordination and mental concentration.

Examples: Vrksasana (Tree Pose), Garudasana (Eagle Pose), Natarajasana (Dancer's Pose).

  • Enhances balance and coordination
  • Builds focus and concentration
  • Strengthens the core and legs
  • Improves mental clarity

Inversions

Inversions reverse the flow of gravity, promoting circulation and improving balance. They can also calm the mind and reduce stress.

Examples: Sirsasana (Headstand), Sarvangasana (Shoulder Stand), Adho Mukha Vrksasana (Handstand).

  • Improves circulation
  • Enhances balance and coordination
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Boosts mental clarity and focus

Relaxation Poses

Relaxation poses allow the body and mind to fully rest and rejuvenate, promoting deep relaxation and stress relief.

Examples: Savasana (Corpse Pose), Supta Baddha Konasana (Reclined Bound Angle Pose).

  • Promotes deep relaxation
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Restores energy levels
