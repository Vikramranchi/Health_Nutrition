Healthy Diets

Explore different diets that can help you lead a healthier lifestyle.

Types of Healthy Diets

Vegetarian Diets

  • Mediterranean Diet: Emphasizes whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and healthy fats.
    Protein: Approximately 15-20% of daily calories. Vitamins: Rich in Vitamins A, C, E, and K from fruits and vegetables.
  • Plant-Based Diet: Focuses on whole, unprocessed plant foods.
    Protein: About 10-15% of daily calories, from legumes, nuts, and seeds. Vitamins: High in B vitamins, especially B6 and folate.
  • Flexitarian Diet: Primarily vegetarian but allows occasional meat and fish.
    Protein: 15-25% of daily calories, depending on meat intake. Vitamins: Balanced intake of various vitamins, especially B12 from occasional meat.
  • Raw Vegan Diet: Consists of unprocessed plant foods that are raw and organic.
    Protein: Approximately 10-15% of daily calories from nuts and seeds. Vitamins: High in Vitamin C and antioxidants from fruits and vegetables.

Non-Vegetarian Diets

  • Keto Diet: A low-carb, high-fat diet that promotes weight loss.
    Protein: About 20-25% of daily calories, primarily from meat and fish. Vitamins: May require supplements for Vitamins A, D, E, and K.
  • Paleo Diet: Focuses on foods that were available to our prehistoric ancestors.
    Protein: Approximately 30-35% of daily calories, high in lean meats. Vitamins: Rich in Vitamins B and C from fruits and vegetables.
  • Atkins Diet: A low-carb diet that emphasizes protein and healthy fats.
    Protein: 25-30% of daily calories, mainly from animal sources. Vitamins: Focus on a variety of foods to ensure a balance of vitamins, including B vitamins.
  • Whole30 Diet: A 30-day program that focuses on whole foods and eliminates sugar, alcohol, grains, and legumes.
    Protein: Varies based on food choices. Vitamins: Aims for a balanced intake of vitamins through diverse whole foods.
